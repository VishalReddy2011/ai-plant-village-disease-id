# Disease Overview: Bacterial Spot
Bacterial spot is a highly destructive bacterial disease affecting peach trees and other *Prunus* species. The disease causes severe leaf loss, shoot damage, and extensive fruit blemishing that can drastically reduce fruit quality and marketability. Under warm, humid conditions—especially in sandy soils—bacterial spot spreads rapidly and can significantly weaken tree vigor by reducing photosynthetic capacity and carbohydrate reserves needed for overwintering. In agricultural diagnostics, machine learning image recognition systems frequently identify bacterial spot lesions with exceptionally high classification accuracy.

## Vulnerable Parts
The disease affects multiple plant structures simultaneously, including **leaves, developing fruit, and tender young shoots**. The bacterium enters plant tissues primarily through natural openings such as stomata or through microscopic wounds and abrasions on the leaf and fruit surface.

## Symptoms on Leaves and Fruit
Leaf symptoms begin as tiny, angular, water-soaked lesions scattered across the leaf surface. As the disease progresses, these lesions darken into purple, brown, or black necrotic spots surrounded by yellow chlorotic tissue. Infected areas often become brittle and detach from the leaf, producing a distinctive **"shot-hole" appearance**. Severe infections can lead to extensive premature defoliation.

Fruit symptoms appear as dark, sunken lesions and rough pitted spots on the fruit skin. Cracking and surface scarring frequently occur, severely reducing fruit quality and creating entry points for secondary fungal pathogens such as brown rot.

## Cause and Spread
The disease is caused by the bacterium *Xanthomonas campestris* pv. *pruni*. The pathogen survives winter inside twig cankers and dormant buds, serving as a source of infection during the following growing season. Warm temperatures, prolonged moisture, and rain splash strongly favor bacterial spread. During spring, bacterial cells are dispersed through wind-driven rain and splashing water, rapidly infecting new tissues, particularly from **petal fall through the first four weeks of growth**.

## Management: Cultural and Mechanical
Long-term suppression depends heavily on cultivar resistance and careful orchard management.

* **Resistant Cultivars:** Planting tolerant or resistant peach varieties is one of the most effective preventative measures, particularly in high-risk environments.
* **Nitrogen Management:** Excessive nitrogen fertilization should be avoided, as rapid succulent growth increases tissue susceptibility.
* **Sandy Soil Management:** Orchards in sandy soils require additional care since blowing sand can create microscopic wounds that facilitate bacterial entry.
* **Canopy Health:** Maintaining balanced vegetative growth helps reduce disease severity and supports long-term orchard resilience.

## Management: Chemical
Chemical control requires careful management because commonly used bactericides may damage peach foliage if improperly applied.

| Active Ingredient | Application Protocol | Efficacy and Safety Notes |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Fixed Copper Formulations** | Apply at **0.5–1.0 oz metallic copper equivalent (MCE) per acre** | Primary bactericide for bacterial spot management; excessive rates may cause chemical injury and defoliation. |
| **Oxytetracycline** | Apply approximately **1.5 lb/acre** in high-volume sprays | Effective antibiotic-based bacterial suppression with minimal foliage damage; repeated applications are often required before rain events. |

Repeated preventative applications are typically necessary for effective suppression, particularly during wet spring periods. Combining oxytetracycline with carefully managed copper programs may reduce disease severity while minimizing chemical injury to foliage.

> **Safety Warning:** Always follow pesticide label instructions regarding dosage, application timing, protective equipment, resistance management, environmental precautions, and pre-harvest intervals to ensure safe and effective disease control.
