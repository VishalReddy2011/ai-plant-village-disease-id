# Disease Overview: Tomato Mosaic Virus
Tomato mosaic virus (ToMV) is a highly infectious and extremely stable viral disease affecting tomato and other solanaceous crops. Due to its exceptional environmental durability, the virus can persist for long periods on contaminated surfaces, plant debris, tools, and seed coats. Infection severely disrupts plant growth, reduces fruit quality, and lowers total yield, particularly when young plants become infected early in development.

## Vulnerable Parts
The virus affects nearly all **vegetative and reproductive tissues**, including **leaves, stems, flowers, and fruit**. Young greenhouse transplants and actively growing plants are especially vulnerable to rapid mechanical transmission during handling and pruning operations.

## Symptoms on Leaves
Infected foliage develops a highly characteristic **mosaic pattern** consisting of alternating light green, dark green, and yellow mottling. Young leaves often become **curled, puckered, distorted, and reduced in size**, sometimes developing a narrow, malformed **fern-like appearance**.

Plants infected during early growth stages become severely stunted, chlorotic, and weak, often producing very few fruit. In some cases, leaves and stems may also develop necrotic streaks, spots, or flecks.

## Symptoms on Fruit
Fruit production is significantly reduced, and affected tomatoes frequently ripen unevenly. Fruit may develop **raised or depressed discoloration rings**, blotchy coloration, or internal browning beneath the skin commonly referred to as **brownwall**.

## Cause and Spread
The disease is caused by **Tomato Mosaic Virus (ToMV)** or the closely related **Tobacco Mosaic Virus (TMV)**, both belonging to the *Tobamovirus* group. These viruses are exceptionally stable and may survive for months or years on contaminated surfaces, dry plant debris, greenhouse structures, tools, stakes, clothing, and seed coats.

Transmission occurs almost entirely through **mechanical contact**, including contaminated hands, pruning tools, stakes, equipment, and plant handling. Unlike many plant viruses, ToMV does **not require an insect vector** for spread. Contaminated irrigation runoff water may also contribute to transmission.

## Management: Cultural and Mechanical
Because infected plants cannot be cured, management depends entirely on prevention, sanitation, and exclusion.

* **Resistant Cultivars:** Plant tomato varieties carrying **ToMV/TMV resistance genes** whenever available.
* **Certified Clean Stock:** Use certified disease-free seed and healthy transplants.
* **Seed Treatment:** Treat seeds using **10% trisodium phosphate (TSP)** solution or dry heat treatment protocols to inactivate virus particles.
* **Worker Hygiene:** Strictly avoid tobacco use near tomato crops and require workers to wash hands thoroughly before handling plants.
* **Plant Removal:** Immediately remove and destroy infected plants; avoid composting diseased material.
* **Tool and Surface Sanitation:** Regularly disinfect pruning tools, stakes, greenhouse structures, and equipment between uses.

## Management: Chemical
There are **no chemical treatments or curative viricides** capable of eliminating ToMV from infected plants. Disease management relies entirely on sanitation and preventative disinfection practices.

| Disinfectant / Method | Product Example | Application Protocol | Efficacy Notes |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Trisodium Phosphate (10%)** | TSP solution | Soak seeds for **15 minutes** before planting | Inactivates viral particles present on seed surfaces. |
| **Germicidal Bleach (1:9 dilution)** | Household bleach | Soak tools for **1 minute** between uses | Highly effective surface disinfectant for pruning tools and equipment. |
| **Nonfat Dry Milk (20% w/v)** | Dry milk solution | Use as hand or tool rinse during pruning | Milk proteins help bind and inactivate viral particles during handling operations. |

Strict sanitation, resistant cultivars, clean seed stock, and rapid removal of infected plants provide the most effective long-term defense against Tomato Mosaic Virus.

> **Safety Warning:** Always follow disinfectant and pesticide label instructions regarding concentration, handling procedures, protective equipment, environmental precautions, and crop safety to ensure effective and safe disease management.
