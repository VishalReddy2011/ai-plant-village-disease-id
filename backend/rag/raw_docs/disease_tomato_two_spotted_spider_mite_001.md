# Disease Overview: Two-Spotted Spider Mite
The two-spotted spider mite (TSSM) is a highly destructive arthropod pest of tomato crops capable of rapidly increasing in population under hot, dry conditions. By piercing plant cells and extracting chlorophyll-rich contents, spider mites significantly reduce photosynthetic capacity, weaken vegetative growth, accelerate leaf aging, and trigger premature defoliation. Severe infestations can drastically reduce yield and, in extreme cases, kill plants entirely.

## Vulnerable Parts
The pest primarily colonizes the **undersides of leaves**, where feeding and reproduction initially occur. Under heavy infestation pressure, mites spread to **upper leaf surfaces, stems, flowers, and fruit**, creating widespread plant damage.

## Symptoms on Leaves
Early symptoms appear as **tiny yellow, white, or brown speckles (stippling)** on the upper surface of leaves caused by feeding damage beneath the leaf tissue. As populations expand, leaves gradually lose chlorophyll and develop yellow, bronze, or reddish-brown discoloration. Severely affected foliage dries, curls, and drops prematurely.

Heavy infestations are commonly associated with the formation of **fine silken webbing**, which may cover leaves, stems, flowers, and fruit. Severe outbreaks often cause stunted growth, widespread defoliation, and major reductions in plant productivity.

## Cause and Spread
The pest is caused by the arachnid **two-spotted spider mite**, *Tetranychus urticae*. Adult mites are extremely small (**~0.5 mm**) and typically appear greenish-yellow with **two dark body spots**. Under hot and dry conditions, spider mites reproduce exceptionally quickly, completing a life cycle in fewer than **10 days** and producing multiple generations per season.

Spider mites overwinter in plant debris, soil, and sheltered surfaces. Spread occurs through **wind currents (ballooning), contaminated tools, workers’ clothing, infested transplants, and plant material**. Outbreaks are strongly associated with **drought stress, excessive nitrogen fertilization, dusty environments, and overuse of broad-spectrum insecticides**, which often eliminate natural predators.

## Management: Cultural and Mechanical
Long-term suppression relies on minimizing plant stress and preserving natural biological control.

* **Adequate Irrigation:** Maintain consistent watering to reduce drought stress, which strongly favors mite outbreaks.
* **Balanced Fertilization:** Avoid excessive nitrogen applications, as lush growth encourages rapid mite reproduction.
* **Dust Management:** Minimize dust accumulation around crops, especially near dirt roads, since dust suppresses beneficial predators.
* **Weed Control:** Manage surrounding weeds carefully, particularly hosts such as bindweed, without triggering mass migration into crops.
* **Field Placement:** Avoid planting tomatoes adjacent to heavily infested weedy fields or susceptible legume forage crops.

## Management: Biological
Biological control can provide highly effective suppression when introduced early.

* **Predatory Mites:** Release beneficial predatory mites such as *Phytoseiulus persimilis*, *Amblyseius fallacis*, or *Amblyseius andersoni*.
* **Conservation of Natural Enemies:** Preserve beneficial organisms including spiders, predatory thrips, minute pirate bugs, and lady beetles by avoiding unnecessary broad-spectrum insecticide use.

## Management: Chemical
Selective miticides (**acaricides**) should be applied immediately when stippling or webbing is first observed. Most miticides require **two applications spaced 5–7 days apart** to control newly emerged mites protected during the egg stage.

| Active Ingredient | Product Example | Application Protocol | Efficacy and Resistance Notes |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Abamectin** | Agri-Mek SC | Apply **1.75–3.5 fl oz/acre** | **Group 6** miticide with strong translaminar activity against mobile mite stages; often requires a non-ionic surfactant (NIS). |
| **Bifenazate** | Acramite 50 WS | Apply **0.75–1.0 lb/acre** | Provides long-lasting contact control of eggs and mobile stages while remaining relatively safe for predatory mites. |
| **Spiromesifen** | Oberon 2SC | Apply **7.0–8.5 fl oz/acre** | **Group 23** miticide targeting eggs and immature stages; compatible with integrated biological control programs. |

Early intervention combined with biological control, irrigation management, and selective miticide use provides the most reliable long-term suppression of two-spotted spider mites.

> **Safety Warning:** Always follow pesticide label instructions regarding dosage, timing, pollinator protection, resistance management, protective equipment, environmental precautions, and pre-harvest intervals to ensure safe and effective pest control.
